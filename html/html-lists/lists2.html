Steps for Scrubbing a Left Heart Cath

  1. Needle puncture as Dr. palpates radial pulse. Sometimes ultrasound is used for this step.
  2. A short 0.035" J-Wire will be inserted through the needle into the radial artery.
  3. Needle is removed, 6 fr Nitinol Sheath is slid over the wire and into the radial artery.
  4. A 0.035" x 150cm J-Wire is inserted into a 100 cm Radial Tig catheter until the J-Wire is at the end of the catheter.
  5. The catheter is inserted into the radial artery through the sheath, and progressed up the radial artery, around the aortic arch. Pin the wire so the catheter can be advanced.
  6. The Dr. will select the Left and Right Coronary Arteries. Inject dye as the Dr moves the x-ray tube around to get different views of the arteries.
  7. When finished, the Dr will remove the catheter over a wire, remove the sheath, and put on a TR Band to stop the patient from bleeding.
